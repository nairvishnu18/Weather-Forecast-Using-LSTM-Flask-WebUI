This Project aims to present a web based interactive visualization and forecasting platform for weather data in the regions of Mumbai and Pune. The weather data used in this project consists of various weather related information of temperature, humidity, rainfall etc. from 2009 to 2020.

Forecast Weather

Actual vs Feels Like

The "Feels-like temperature," specifically relating to when its values are greater than the actual temperature, is a measure of how hot it really feels for a human when the relative humidity is factored in. The average temperature for all time in the region of Mumbai is around 29 and 30 degree celsius where as for Pune it is around 28 and 30 degrees. The Feels like temperature is related to the humidity and it significantly varies around +- 3 oC.

Effect of Rainfall on Temeprature

Depending on the Humidity level, a rain can cool or make an environment feel warmer. As average temperatures at the Earth's surface rise, more evaporation occurs, which, in turn, increases overall precipitation. Therefore, a warming climate is expected to increase precipitation. In regions of Mumbai where the humidity increases, it is expected to increase the level of precipitation.

Humidity and Precipitation Variation

Humidity indicates the likelihood for precipitation, dew, or fog to be present. The amount of water vapor needed to achieve saturation increases as the temperature increases. However, the longer it rains, the more the humidity will increase because of the air constantly drawing the water.

Rainfall Variation

India's Monsoon has shown significant changes over the past few years. The annual showers has changed due to climate change especially in past few decades. There has been increase in rainy days especially from June to September in overall Maharashtra. Mumbai has received a average precipitation level upto 0.6mm and Pune of 0.28mm over past few years.
